# [Psychology Source] Behavioral Economics & Negotiation Strategy

This document contains specialized behavioral psychology insights to be used as a \"Source\" for high-end negotiation proposals for Changwon Unicity.

## 🧠 1. Core Cognitive Biases (Kahneman & Tversky)
Academic principles that explain non-rational human behavior during bargaining.

### 1.1 Anchoring Effect (닻 내리기 효과)
- **Concept**: The human tendency to rely too heavily on the first piece of information offered (the \"anchor\").
- **Application**: The initial price offered (or the \'Original Sale Price\' reference) sets the benchmark. Even if unrealistic, it pulls the final agreed price towards itself.
- **Strategy**: Always be the one to set a firm, data-backed anchor first to control the negotiation range.

### 1.2 Framing Effect (프레이밍 효과)
- **Concept**: The way information is presented (\"framed\") significantly affects the decision.
- **Application**:
- *Positive Frame*: \"You are gaining a 200M KRW profit compared to the original sale price.\"
- *Negative Frame*: \"You are losing the opportunity to secure this prime unit before the 2026 price surge.\"
- **Strategy**: Frame the deal as an \"avoidance of loss\" for sellers and a \"unique gain\" for buyers.

### 1.3 Loss Aversion (손실 회피 향성)
- **Concept**: The pain of losing is twice as powerful as the joy of gaining.
- **Application**: Sellers are more motivated by the fear of their property devaluing than the potential of future profit.
- **Strategy**: Highlight cost-of-waiting or potential tax/policy risks to trigger loss aversion in the counterparty.

# [Psychology Source] Real Estate Psychology & Market Behavior

This specialized source focuses on psychological principles unique to the real estate market, moving beyond general behavioral economics into the specific \"psychology of home.\"

## 🏠 1. The Endowment Effect (WTA vs. WTP Gap)
Academic research (Kahneman et al.) shows that sellers value their property significantly higher simply because they own it.

- **The Gap**: Willingness to Accept (WTA) by sellers is almost always higher than Willingness to Pay (WTP) by buyers.
- **Cause**: Loss Aversion. Selling a home feels like \"losing\" a part of one\'s identity or history.
- **Negotiation Tactic**:
- *For Buyers*: Help the seller \"dis-identify\" with the property by focusing on the future (the new owner\'s plans) rather than the past.
- *For Agents*: Use \"Comparative Market Analysis (CMA)\" to provide an objective external reference point that diffuses the subjective endowment value.

## 🧱 2. Sunk Cost Fallacy in Housing
Many sellers confuse \"Money Spent\" with \"Market Value.\"

- **The Bias**: \"I spent 50M KRW on premium interior renovations, so the house must be worth 50M KRW more.\"
- **The Reality**: Market value is determined by what a buyer is willing to pay, and some renovations actually decrease the potential buyer pool.
- **Negotiation Tactic**: Use \"Value-in-Use vs. Value-in-Exchange\" logic. Explain that while the renovation was valuable for the seller\'s *use*, the market *exchange* value follows supply/demand laws of the complex.
